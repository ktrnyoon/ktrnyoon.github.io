Bridging photonics
and electronics,
end-to-end.

From device design to full experimental system implementation — I work at the intersection of integrated photonics, non-Hermitian optics, and quantum photonic devices at KAIST.

The next frontier of AI hardware lies at the boundary of light and electronics. My research aims to build photonic–electronic hybrid platforms that combine the programmability of electronics with the ultra-high bandwidth and energy efficiency of photonics, enabling device functionalities beyond conventional computing.

Research

Current Directions
📷 research-ep.jpg — exceptional point / photoluminescence image
Non-Hermitian Photonics
Exceptional Points in Photonic Structures
Designing and experimentally demonstrating exceptional points in passive waveguides and InAs/GaAs quantum dot membrane structures. Work spans COMSOL/RCWA simulation through cryogenic angle-resolved photoluminescence and Fourier-plane imaging, conducted at University of Würzburg in collaboration with KAIST and ETRI.
Quantum Dots ARPL / TRPL COMSOL · RCWA Cryogenic Optics
📷 research-comb.jpg — microcomb / ring resonator setup
Nonlinear Integrated Photonics
Microcombs & Frequency Conversion
Full PIC measurement platform setup for nonlinear optics and microcomb generation. Dispersion-engineered ring resonators, broadband SHG-based spectral transfer, and second-harmonic microcomb transfer in concentric ring resonators. MPW fabrication via Ligentec AN350.
Microcombs SHG Ring Resonators Ligentec AN350 gdsfactory
📷 research-syn.jpg — 2D material / device microscopy image
Optoelectronics · Neuromorphic
2D-Material Optoelectronic Synapses
Design, fabrication, and characterization of van der Waals heterostructure synaptic devices (MoS₂/InSe) for neuromorphic computing. Demonstrated tunable positive and negative post-synaptic currents enabling highly accurate spiking neural networks. Published in Advanced Functional Materials (Front Cover, 2026).
2D Materials vdW Heterostructure Neuromorphic SNN
